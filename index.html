My name is Ariana Verduzco and I am currently a fourth year undergraduate student at the University of California, Riverside working towards a Bachelors of Science in Neuroscience. The past four years that I have spent at UCR have been instrumental in my personal and professional development, as I have been presented with a plethora of opportunities that have guided my experience as a student and worker.

I have participated in several clubs that have piqued my interests, including Physical Therapy (PhyT) Club, Neuroscience Club, and the Migraine Initiative. Each of these clubs cultivated my interests and brought me into circles of like-minded people who encouraged me to pursue my goals. I also co-founded the UCR Chapter of Haven Neighborhood Services, and currently serve as the club's Treasurer. The club is very personal to me because it centers around aiding low-income communities in Los Angeles that are affected by the increasing living costs caused by gentrification. Because I am from Los Angeles, I see first-hand the detrimental effects gentrification has on low-income communities that are predominantly comprised of minorities. I am proud of taking the initiative to directly help issues in my community that I see have direct negative effects on those around me.

I am currently seeking job opportunities in the medical field, preferably as a medical scribe or medical coder. I am working towards an EMT license and hope to gain experince as an EMT worker, because in the future I plan to apply to Physician's Assistant programs. I am hoping to gain as much experience as possible before I enter the competitive field, and I am confident that I will achieve my goals and eventually become a PA.
